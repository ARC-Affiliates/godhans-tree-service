Seasonal Tree Care Tips for Jacksonville's Climate

Jacksonville's subtropical climate creates a year-round tree care schedule. Understanding seasonal needs helps you maintain healthy trees and prevent emergency situations.

Spring (March-May): This is prime time for structural pruning before new growth begins. Inspect trees for winter damage, especially following any ice storms. Apply mulch to retain moisture as temperatures rise, but keep it away from trunk bases to prevent rot.

Summer (June-August): Monitor trees for drought stress during hot, dry periods. Deep watering is more beneficial than frequent shallow watering. Watch for pest activity that increases in warm weather. Hurricane preparedness includes removing dead branches and securing loose limbs before storm season peaks.

Fall (September-November): This optimal time for major tree work allows cuts to heal before winter dormancy. Remove hazardous trees before winter storms. Fall is also ideal for planting new trees, giving roots time to establish before summer heat.

Winter (December-February): Deciduous trees reveal structural issues when leaves drop. This is excellent timing for assessments and major pruning. Ice storm damage requires prompt professional attention to prevent further breakage.
