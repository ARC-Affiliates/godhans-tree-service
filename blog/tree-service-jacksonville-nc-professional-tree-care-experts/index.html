How Much Does It Cost to Cut Down a Tree in NC?

Tree removal costs in North Carolina vary significantly based on multiple factors, with prices ranging from $200 for small trees under 30 feet to $3,000 or more for large specimens exceeding 80 feet. In Jacksonville specifically, average tree removal costs fall between $400 and $1,500 for typical residential trees, though complex situations involving power lines, nearby structures, or difficult access can increase prices substantially.

Several key factors influence tree removal pricing in our area. Tree size remains the primary cost determinant—larger trees require more time, specialized equipment, and additional labor. A 30-foot Bradford pear might cost $400-$600 to remove, while a 70-foot loblolly pine could range from $1,200-$2,200. Tree location significantly impacts pricing as well; trees near homes, power lines, or in tight spaces require careful rigging and sectional removal, increasing labor time and complexity. What Do Tree Services Charge Per Hour?

Understanding hourly rates helps Jacksonville homeowners budget for tree care services and compare quotes effectively. Professional tree service companies in North Carolina typically charge between $75 and $200 per hour depending on the specific service, equipment required, and crew size needed. However, most tree service providers quote projects on a per-job basis rather than hourly rates, as this approach provides clearer pricing for both parties.

Basic tree trimming and pruning services generally fall on the lower end of the hourly spectrum, around $75-$125 per hour for a two-person crew with standard equipment. These services include canopy thinning, dead branch removal, and routine maintenance that requires bucket trucks or basic climbing equipment. More complex work involving large tree removal, crane-assisted operations, or hazardous tree situations can reach $150-$200+ per hour due to specialized equipment costs and increased labor requirements.

The best tree service Jacksonville companies often structure pricing around project completion rather than hourly billing. This approach protects homeowners from unexpected costs if work takes longer than anticipated and incentivizes efficient completion. A flat-rate quote for removing a specific tree or trimming designated specimens provides budget certainty and eliminates concerns about crews working slowly to increase billable hours.

When comparing tree service costs, consider what's included in quoted prices. Comprehensive services should include debris removal, site cleanup, and disposal of wood and branches. Some companies offer firewood delivery from removed trees or wood chip mulch at no additional charge. Others may charge separately for stump grinding, root removal, or hauling services.
