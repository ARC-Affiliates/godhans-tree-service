How Much Does It Cost to Cut Down a Tree in NC?

Tree removal costs in North Carolina vary considerably based on multiple factors, with Jacksonville prices generally reflecting regional averages. Small trees under 30 feet typically cost between $150-$500 for removal, medium trees (30-60 feet) range from $500-$1,200, and large trees exceeding 60 feet can cost $1,200-$2,500 or more.

Several variables influence final pricing beyond simple height measurements. Tree location significantly affects cost—trees near power lines, structures, or in tight spaces require more careful rigging and time. Species matters as well; hardwoods like oak demand more labor than softer pines. Tree health also impacts pricing, as diseased or dead trees may be more unpredictable and dangerous to remove.
