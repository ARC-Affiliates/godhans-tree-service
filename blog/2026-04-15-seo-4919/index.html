How Much Does It Cost to Cut Down a Tree in NC?

Tree removal costs in North Carolina vary considerably based on size, location, accessibility, and species. Understanding these pricing factors helps Jacksonville homeowners budget appropriately and identify reasonable estimates.

For small trees under 30 feet tall, expect to pay between $150 and $500. These smaller removals typically require minimal equipment and can often be completed in a few hours. Medium trees ranging from 30 to 60 feet generally cost $500 to $1,200, depending on proximity to structures and complexity of the job. What Do Tree Services Charge Per Hour?

Understanding hourly rates versus project-based pricing helps Jacksonville property owners evaluate estimates and ensure fair pricing for tree service work.

Most professional tree services, including Godhans, charge project-based flat rates rather than hourly fees because tree work involves specialized equipment, multiple crew members, insurance considerations, and disposal costs that make hourly pricing impractical. However, understanding the underlying labor costs provides valuable context.

When tree services do charge hourly rates—typically for consulting, minor trimming, or ongoing maintenance contracts—Jacksonville-area rates generally range from $75 to $150 per hour for ground crew members and $150 to $300+ per hour for certified arborists. These rates reflect the specialized training, expensive equipment, comprehensive insurance coverage, and significant liability involved in professional tree work.

Project-based pricing better serves homeowners because it provides cost certainty, accounts for all equipment and disposal needs, includes insurance and liability coverage, and allows for accurate budgeting without surprises. Reputable tree service companies provide detailed written estimates that break down labor, equipment, disposal, stump grinding if requested, and site cleanup and restoration.

Choosing the Best Tree Service Jacksonville Has to Offer

Selecting the right tree service provider protects your property, ensures quality work, and provides peace of mind throughout the process. Jacksonville homeowners should prioritize several critical factors when evaluating tree service companies.

First, verify proper licensing and insurance. North Carolina doesn't require state-level tree service licensing, but reputable companies carry comprehensive general liability insurance (minimum $1 million coverage) and workers' compensation insurance for all employees. Always request proof of current insurance certificates before work begins.

Look for certified arborists with ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) credentials, which demonstrate specialized knowledge and ongoing education. Check references and online reviews from Jacksonville-area customers, verify business longevity and local reputation, and ensure the company uses professional-grade equipment maintained to safety standards.
